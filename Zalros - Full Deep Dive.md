Zalros and his brother Benadeer were two scholars who spent many years traveling across Teldire. From corner to corner they combed countless musty libraries, met wizened and silent hermi​ts, and have eluded creatures one dare not even name. To this end, they poured their hearts and souls into their research, coming to understand the fundamental forces of magic and the arcane on an impeccable level.

During one of their adventures through the Southern realms, Benadeer caught an foul illness that had been plaguing a neighbour town; fearful of catching the sickness themselves, no apothecary, priest, or medical practitioner dared to attempt treatment of Zalros' quickly deteriorating brother.

Knowing his brother's life would end if they didn't take dramatic action, Zalros hastily enscribed the ground with runes while reciting words he imagined he'd never speak aloud. As the final syllable left his lips, the air suddenly fell silent - every hair on his body had suddenly come to attention as he felt paralyzed by... fear? Reverence? It was an unfamilliar feeling to Zalros, but one that confirmed that his spell had worked as planned.

Although he could percieve no entity with him, he knew his plea would receive an answer. His entire life's knowledge of the Arcane and everything he had spent his years realizing — this was his offer. In return, he asked to function as a conduit of It's energy, so that he may channel it into his brother to purify him.
