# Session 2 - Mutual Ache for Something More

## Main Session Notes

We returned to the party in front of the two figures in the room they had just unlocked the session prior. Inside the room - which at one point in time appeared to be an old storage room - stood two figurines on pedestals, similar to the party's existing three. ^storage-room-figurines

After awakening [[Oswald]] and [[Lacey]], the party discovered the name of the green figure was **[[Peridot]]**, and the name of the black figure was [[Mars]]. The party awakened [[Peridot]] first, who seemed confused and upset as to why [[Samwell]] was not there. The party then awoke [[Mars]], and they had a discussion to explain the situation. The party explained [[Samwell]]'s fate, and that the building had likely been broken into given the bodies they encountered earlier. [[Lacey]] and [[Oswald]] struggled a bit with the concept of death, given it was the first time they had really encountered it. ^peridot-mars-awakened

After some further discussion, [[Mars]] seemed excited to clear out the basement and see some action again after some time. Mars recalled that there was likely a web glyph against the back wall, and that they could use it to clear the remaining Helmed Horror. [[Elara]] and Mars took the lead, with [[Shamash]] following for backup. Elara took cover on the other side of the hall, and once in position, threw some birdseed to draw the attention of the Helmed Horror.

Immediately, [[Peridot]] jumped in and began fiercely attacking the Helmed Horror, undeterred by its attacks. The Helmed Horror responded by striking Peridot away, and then attacking the party shortly after. [[Brog]] summoned a small version of Jerry, and shortly after, [[Zalros]] grabbed Brog to teleport back further down the hallway; Brog felt nauseous from this. Shortly after, [[Shamash]] fired a Ray of Frost at the Helmed Horror before making a decisive critical blow against the beast. Having encountered one of these creations before, Shamash went for an opening in the armor and severed the energy holding the construct together in a single, decisive blow. [[Mars]] was upset that this was done without him seeing, but was glad the party could bring back his love for action. ^helmed-horror-fight

[[Peridot]] continued to investigate alone, leaving the party and heading back to the beginning of the tower. [[Brog]] returned to the storage room where they had located [[Peridot]] and [[Mars]], noting that there appeared to be a teleportation circle at the back of the room. Brog and [[Zalros]] studied it for a brief time, and Brog seemed to be able to pattern-match the arcane symbols, completing the circle with some ether chalk. The party decided it was likely best to leave this teleportation circle for another time, as they were already quite winded from returning from a demiplane. ^storage-room-teleportation-circle

The party continued up the hallway where the Helmed Horror had been, discovering a large museum-like room containing swords, scrolls, and other curiosities. [[Drakka]] found a scroll they recognized as a valuable treasure from their culture - the Scroll of the Three Principles. It appeared worn down, torn, and slightly soaked by water, but [[Elara]] decided to attempt a dexterous recovery. Elara rolled a **Natural 1**, completely crumbling the scroll. ^scroll-destroyed

> [!danger] Notable Loss
> **Scroll of the Three Principles** - A culturally significant relic from [[Drakka]]'s people, destroyed when [[Elara]] rolled a Natural 1 attempting to recover it.

[[Artemis]], playing about with a sword in the displays, struck [[Drakka]] for a small bit of damage. Drakka disarmed [[Artemis]], who claimed to have been only playing at being an adventurer like the party. Looking further at the swords around the room, [[Shamash]] found a longsword encased in a locked glass display. [[Elara]] attempted to pick the lock but did not succeed; this melted Elara's thieves' tools and caused the lock to deal some shrapnel damage to those in the area. ^museum-room

[[Zalros]] took a moment to identify the lock and noted that it was indeed a standard arcane lock. It required being struck by either a magic weapon, having the intended key open it, or being successfully lockpicked. Examining the room further, the party found photos of the sword being held by many - first by a centaur, then an elf, and finally a Celestial. Then, a scroll was found depicting the sword displayed in front of some sort of temple, beside a very specific star constellation - the sign of the Swordsman (forever known as the Sign of the Nose). Zalros drew the constellation on the glass case using his Illuminator's Tattoo, and the glass case opened. ^longsword-found

> [!tip] Loot Acquired
> **Longsword +3** - Requires attunement. Three gem slots (500 gp each) that determine damage type per hit. Normal rocks placed in the slots transform into gems over 1d4 months. Quality varies by rock.
> The party has also, for some reason, decided that this sword is Jewish and celebrates Hanukkah.

[[Brog]] remembered to check if the ants were okay after this encounter, and they claimed they were just fine. Speaking further with Brog, they let him know that they used to be housekeepers for [[Samwell]]; they had stored their jewels upstairs before they were turned into ants, and would like to get them back before they left. As the ants continued their story, Brog climbed up the wizard's tower to the room they were discussing, where he grew suspicious of their account. Brog decided to return to the main room of the wizard's tower with the rest of the party to discuss the situation with everybody else.

After some back and forth, the ants eventually confessed that they were robbers, and had triggered a cursed object while looting that turned them into ants. They pleaded that they meant no harm - just needed something to buy food and survive. The party still doubted their story, at which time the ants began to bite [[Brog]]'s hands. Brog crushed the ants in his hand after they cried "Just kill us!", however, Brog opened his hand to find the ants seemed quite fine. The ants quickly dropped to the floor and scurried to the door; [[Elara]] chased after them and gave them a firm "squish" with her boot, but they seemed unharmed and just slightly pressed into the dirt outside.

> [!quote] Memorable Quote
> "Try all you want, but that wizard made us immortal so we'd be ants forever!"

After that, [[Brog]] quickly grabbed an empty vial and stuffed the ants inside, corking the bottle with a big stick of chalk. ^ants-bottled

Shortly after returning with the sealed bottle of ants, the party received a message from [[Samwell]]. Samwell advised that he was sending a longer message this time; he offered a map to the shrine of the Jeering Sparrow to [[Zalros]], and gave the party further lore on Jerry. Samwell also advised that the two ants were members of a nefarious criminal ring that extorted creatures into the exotic pet trade market, and were not to be freed under any means necessary. The party placed them back inside the ant colony that they came from, ramming the sealed bottle into the hole to rest alongside the other ants. ^samwell-message

> [!info] Lore Drop
> [[Samwell]] provided instructions to [[Brog]] on how to perform the spell "Grant Existence," which grants existence to his figurine carvings. Brog confirmed it would be a very long and challenging process to master, but would work towards it in the coming year.

^grant-existence-learned

After [[Samwell]]'s messages concluded, the party confirmed that they should make their way to the dugout they were originally tasked to gather bodies from - it was only about a 20-minute walk from the wizard's tower after all.

Upon arriving at the dugout, the party found the entrance barred off, with signs out front warning that the territory had been sealed off by the guild, and no one was to enter. Assuming their job had already been accounted for, the party decided to make their way back to Gosslupe proper after their time away. ^dugout-sealed

---

After the party arrived at the gates of Gosslupe City, they discovered a pair of Sunderland folks ahead with their cart - a boy and his father by the looks of things, hauling a load of geese. As they left, [[Shamash]] and [[Zalros]] noticed they had dropped a crate of eggs, so they scurried off to grab it and return it to them. The boy thanked them for returning the eggs, and the party learned that they intended to fatten one of their geese up to win the "fattest animal" contest. They were using cockatrice eggs to feed the goose - gifted to the boy and his father by a woman they had met along the way. ^gosslupe-arrival

During this time, [[Elara]] had taken time to visit the market in the city. She stumbled upon two elderly goblin ladies selling an assortment of baked goods and warm food at their stall, "Gobblin' it Up." Elara purchased some food and ended up with a massive bowl of stew that the goblin ladies sold her for 1 CP. Elara made her way to a table and began eating, while [[Brog]] made his way to where his mother may have been running her stall. ^gobblin-it-up

Upon arrival, [[Brog]] didn't see his mother in her usual spot, but instead found a vendor selling pigs and piglets. Brog decided to purchase a truffle-hunting pig for his farm and spoke with the pigs directly before choosing one. Brog started to make his way out of the city when he walked by [[Elara]], who had reunited with [[Zalros]] and [[Shamash]]. They began to catch up on what had happened while eating briefly, before Brog left to tell his mother that he was safe. ^pig-purchased

During this, [[Drakka]] made their way to a perfume shop to purchase a gift for somebody important who was visiting them very soon. Drakka had received word that somebody very close to them would be visiting the Auction House in the coming day for their birthday, and Drakka wanted to get them something nice. Drakka picked out a nice perfume from a shop, but was disappointed to discover its alarming price of almost 40 GP. Opting for something cheaper, Drakka purchased a single flower from the shop and left. ^drakka-flower

Around this time, [[Zalros]] had also received the map to the temple of the Jeering Sparrow. The map, suspiciously in the shape of his birthsign, outlined the resting points of a large golem made by giants. It was a large, mobile sacred temple that slowly made its way across Teldire. Its name was "The Kiln" - every 300 years, it rested at a given point along its path, and would only continue after a subsequent rest of another 300 years. Colonies had gathered around its resting points historically, and it had shaped the path of Teldire and its cities somewhat. The temple of the Jeering Sparrow rested at the very heart of this golem. ^kiln-map-received

[[Elara]] visited a general store stall and purchased a bundle of arrows as well as some "mystery scrolls" - their effects were unknown, but because of that, they were being sold for cheap. Elara grabbed a purple and green scroll. ^mystery-scrolls-purchased

[[Elara]] then made her way to the adventurer's guild, encountering a group of young and brash adventurers who quickly received a scolding from **[[Jarvis]]**, an experienced adventurer who gave his calling stone to Elara and promised to find some information on Asta Vale, Elara's sister. ^jarvis-met

![[Jarvis.png|150]]

[[Brog]] made his way back to the farm and found things in a state of disrepair; he found [[Brog's Mom|his mother]] worried sick, as they had been gone for a few days, and she had been busy dealing with the farm and some local troublemaker kids who were up to antics. Brog settled in at home briefly, promising to help right the farm again and advising his mother to rest after her days of worry. ^brog-farm-return

[[Zalros]] was looking to sell some goods that he had found in the demiplane and made his way to a local curiosities and alchemist vendor. Zalros encountered [[Chisum]], a charming young man who seemed to take a quick interest in Zalros. Zalros, being quite oblivious to the situation initially, walked through the conversation with blissful ignorance, almost insinuating that he and [[Shamash]] were "very good friends." [[Chisum]] invited Zalros over to his house later for further investigation of some goods that Zalros had, and Zalros agreed to meet him soon to further identify some of the things that had been found. ^chisum-met

![[Chisum.png|150]]

Returning to [[Drakka]], they found themselves outside of the auction house awaiting Rosetta. Rosetta stepped out of a horse-drawn carriage outside of the auction house with her sisters, Georgette and Janet. They made their way inside the auction house, where Rosetta bid outlandish amounts of money on items. ^auction-house

Eventually, a coffin was dragged on stage; Rosetta claimed that this was the very reason she was there - to purchase this coffin. As the coffin was dragged onto the stage, it began to shake and rattle violently! The chains and bindings sealing it shut began to fall off, and eventually the coffin cracked open. What might be inside? We'll have to wait until the next session to find out! That's where we'll end things for this time! ^coffin-cliffhanger

> [!warning] Cliffhanger
> A coffin Rosetta specifically traveled to acquire began violently shaking, shed its chains, and cracked open at center stage. Whatever is inside did not want to stay put.

## Next Session Recap

When last we left our party, they had just finished clearing the depths of Samwell's tower - awakening two new companions in [[Peridot]] and [[Mars]], felling the final Helmed Horror with a decisive strike from [[Shamash]], and uncovering a magnificent longsword sealed behind an arcane lock. Not everything went so smoothly, of course. A culturally priceless scroll crumbled to dust in [[Elara]]'s hands, a pair of "innocent" ants turned out to be immortal criminals, and [[Samwell]] himself reached out from afar with gifts and warnings alike; a map for [[Zalros]] tracing the path of an ancient, wandering colossus called the Kiln, and instructions for [[Brog]] on a spell that could one day breathe life into his carvings.

With the tower behind them and the dugout already sealed off by the guild, the party made their way back to Gosslupe and scattered into the city. [[Elara]] found herself a bowl of stew for a single copper piece, courtesy of two lovely goblin ladies at "Gobblin' it Up," before picking up some mystery scrolls and a new contact at the adventurer's guild - a seasoned adventurer named [[Jarvis]], who promised to look into the whereabouts of her sister, Asta Vale. [[Brog]] picked out a truffle-hunting pig for the family farm and returned home to find his mother worried sick and the property in rough shape. [[Zalros]], meanwhile, wandered into a curiosities shop and met [[Chisum]] - a charming young vendor who took quite the interest in him, though Zalros remained cheerfully oblivious. And somewhere along the way, a Sunderland boy and his father were seen feeding cockatrice eggs to a goose for a contest.

But the night ended on a very different note; [[Drakka]], waiting outside the auction house, watched as Rosetta arrived in a horse-drawn carriage with her sisters for her birthday. Inside, Rosetta bid lavishly on item after item; but it was the final lot that she truly came for. A coffin, dragged onto the stage in chains. As the room watched, those chains began to snap - the bindings fell away. The coffin shook, rattled, and cracked open. Whatever was sealed inside... did not want to stay sealed. And that is where we pick up tonight!
