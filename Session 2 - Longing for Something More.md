We returned to the party in front of the two figures in the room they had just unlocked the session prior. Inside the room - which at one point in time appeared to be an old storage room - stood two figurines on pedestals, similar to the party's existing three.

After awakening **Oswald** and **Lacey**, the party discovered the name of the green figure was **Peridot**, and the name of the black figure was **Mars**. The party awakened Peridot first, who seemed confused and upset as to why Samwell was not there. The party then awoke Mars, and they had a discussion to explain the situation. The party explained Samwell's fate, and that the building had likely been broken into given the bodies they encountered earlier. Lacey and Oswald struggled a bit with the concept of death, given it was the first time they had really encountered it.

After some further discussion, Mars seemed excited to clear out the basement and see some action again after some time. Mars recalled that there was likely a web glyph against the back wall, and that they could use it to clear the remaining Helmed Horror. Elara and Mars took the lead, with Shamash following for backup. Elara took cover on the other side of the hall, and once in position, threw some birdseed to draw the attention of the Helmed Horror.

Immediately, Peridot jumped in and began fiercely attacking the Helmed Horror, undeterred by its attacks. The Helmed Horror responded by striking Peridot away, and then attacking the party shortly after. Brog summoned a small version of Jerry, and shortly after, Zalros grabbed Brog to teleport back further down the hallway; Brog felt nauseous from this. Shortly after, Shamash fired a Ray of Frost at the Helmed Horror before making a decisive critical blow against the beast. Having encountered one of these creations before, Shamash went for an opening in the armor and severed the energy holding the construct together in a single, decisive blow. Mars was upset that this was done without him seeing, but was glad the party could bring back his love for action.

Peridot continued to investigate alone, leaving the party and heading back to the beginning of the tower. Brog returned to the storage room where they had located Peridot and Mars, noting that there appeared to be a teleportation circle at the back of the room. Brog and Zalros studied it for a brief time, and Brog seemed to be able to pattern-match the arcane symbols, completing the circle with some ether chalk. The party decided it was likely best to leave this teleportation circle for another time, as they were already quite winded from returning from a demiplane.

The party continued up the hallway where the Helmed Horror had been, discovering a large museum-like room containing swords, scrolls, and other curiosities. Drakka found a scroll they recognized as a valuable treasure from their culture - the Scroll of the Three Principles. It appeared worn down, torn, and slightly soaked by water, but Elara decided to attempt a dexterous recovery. Elara rolled a **Natural 1**, completely crumbling the scroll.

Artemis, playing about with a sword in the displays, struck Drakka for a small bit of damage. Drakka disarmed Artemis, who claimed to have been only playing at being an adventurer like the party. Looking further at the swords around the room, Shamash found a longsword encased in a locked glass display. Elara attempted to pick the lock but did not succeed; this melted Elara's thieves' tools and caused the lock to deal some shrapnel damage to those in the area.

Zalros took a moment to identify the lock and noted that it was indeed a standard arcane lock. It required being struck by either a magic weapon, having the intended key open it, or being successfully lockpicked. Examining the room further, the party found photos of the sword being held by many - first by a centaur, then an elf, and finally a Celestial. Then, a scroll was found depicting the sword displayed in front of some sort of temple, beside a very specific star constellation - the sign of the Swordsman (forever known as the Sign of the Nose). Zalros drew the constellation on the glass case using his Illuminator's Tattoo, and the glass case opened. The party was granted access to the longsword - Zalros took some time to identify it, eventually discovering the following:

> **Longsword +3**
> Requires attunement
> Depending on the gems submitted into its slots equal to 500 gp, it deals different damage.
> You can choose the damage type between three gems on every hit made.
> Normal rocks, after 1d4 months in the slots, turn into a gem; quality varies by rock.
> The party has also, for some reason, decided that this sword is Jewish and celebrates Hanukkah.

Brog remembered to check if the ants were okay after this encounter, and they claimed they were just fine. Speaking further with Brog, they let him know that they used to be housekeepers for Samwell; they had stored their jewels upstairs before they were turned into ants, and would like to get them back before they left. As the ants continued their story, Brog climbed up the wizard's tower to the room they were discussing, where he grew suspicious of their account. Brog decided to return to the main room of the wizard's tower with the rest of the party to discuss the situation with everybody else.

After some back and forth, the ants eventually confessed that they were robbers, and had triggered a cursed object while looting that turned them into ants. They pleaded that they meant no harm - just needed something to buy food and survive. The party still doubted their story, at which time the ants began to bite Brog's hands. Brog crushed the ants in his hand after they cried "Just kill us!", however, Brog opened his hand to find the ants seemed quite fine. The ants quickly dropped to the floor and scurried to the door; Elara chased after them and gave them a firm "squish" with her boot, but they seemed unharmed and just slightly pressed into the dirt outside. The ants exclaimed "Try all you want, but that wizard made us immortal so we'd be ants forever!"

After that, Brog quickly grabbed an empty vial and stuffed the ants inside, corking the bottle with a big stick of chalk. Shortly after returning with the sealed bottle of ants, the party received a message from Samwell. Samwell advised that he was sending a longer message this time; he offered a map to the shrine of the Jeering Sparrow to Zalros, and gave the party further lore on Jerry. Samwell also advised that the two ants were members of a nefarious criminal ring that extorted creatures into the exotic pet trade market, and were not to be freed under any means necessary. The party placed them back inside the ant colony that they came from, ramming the sealed bottle into the hole to rest alongside the other ants.

Samwell also provided instructions to Brog on how to perform the spell "Grant Existence," which grants existence to his figurine carvings. Brog confirmed that it would be a very long and challenging process to master this spell, but would work towards it in the coming year. After Samwell's messages concluded, the party confirmed that they should make their way to the dugout they were originally tasked to gather bodies from - it was only about a 20-minute walk from the wizard's tower after all.

Upon arriving at the dugout, the party found the entrance barred off, with signs out front warning that the territory had been sealed off by the guild, and no one was to enter. Assuming their job had already been accounted for, the party decided to make their way back to Gosslupe proper after their time away.

After the party arrived at the gates of Gosslupe City, they discovered a pair of Sunderland folks ahead with their cart - a boy and his father by the looks of things, hauling a load of geese. As they left, Shamash and Zalros noticed they had dropped a crate of eggs, so they scurried off to grab it and return it to them. The boy thanked them for returning the eggs, and the party learned that they intended to fatten one of their geese up to win the "fattest animal" contest. They were using cockatrice eggs to feed the goose - gifted to the boy and his father by a woman they had met along the way.

During this time, Elara had taken time to visit the market in the city. She stumbled upon two elderly goblin ladies selling an assortment of baked goods and warm food at their stall, "Gobblin' it Up." Elara purchased some food and ended up with a massive bowl of stew that the goblin ladies sold her for 1 CP. Elara made her way to a table and began eating, while Brog made his way to where his mother may have been running her stall.

Upon arrival, Brog didn't see his mother in her usual spot, but instead found a vendor selling pigs and piglets. Brog decided to purchase a truffle-hunting pig for his farm and spoke with the pigs directly before choosing one. Brog started to make his way out of the city when he walked by Elara, who had reunited with Zalros and Shamash. They began to catch up on what had happened while eating briefly, before Brog left to tell his mother that he was safe.

During this, Drakka made their way to a perfume shop to purchase a gift for somebody important who was visiting them very soon. Drakka had received word that somebody very close to them would be visiting the Auction House in the coming day for their birthday, and Drakka wanted to get them something nice. Drakka picked out a nice perfume from a shop, but was disappointed to discover its alarming price of almost 40 GP. Opting for something cheaper, Drakka purchased a single flower from the shop and left.

Around this time, Zalros had also received the map to the temple of the Jeering Sparrow. The map, suspiciously in the shape of his birthsign, outlined the resting points of a large golem made by giants. It was a large, mobile sacred temple that slowly made its way across Teldire. Its name was "The Kiln" - every 300 years, it rested at a given point along its path, and would only continue after a subsequent rest of another 300 years. Colonies had gathered around its resting points historically, and it had shaped the path of Teldire and its cities somewhat. The temple of the Jeering Sparrow rested at the very heart of this golem.

Elara visited a general store stall and purchased a bundle of arrows as well as some "mystery scrolls" - their effects were unknown, but because of that, they were being sold for cheap. Elara grabbed a purple and green scroll. Elara then made her way to the adventurer's guild, encountering a group of young and brash adventurers who quickly received a scolding from **Jarvis**, an experienced adventurer who gave his calling stone to Elara and promised to find some information on Asta Vale, Elara's sister.

Brog made his way back to the farm and found things in a state of disrepair; he found his mother worried sick, as they had been gone for a few days, and his mother had been busy dealing with the farm and some local troublemaker kids who were up to antics. Brog settled in at home briefly, promising to help right the farm again and advising his mother to rest after her days of worry.

Zalros was looking to sell some goods that he had found in the demiplane and made his way to a local curiosities and alchemist vendor. Zalros encountered Chisum, a charming young man who seemed to take a quick interest in Zalros. Zalros, being quite oblivious to the situation initially, walked through the conversation with blissful ignorance, almost insinuating that he and Shamash were "very good friends." Chisum invited Zalros over to his house later for further investigation of some goods that Zalros had, and Zalros agreed to meet him soon to further identify some of the things that had been found.

Returning to Drakka, they found themselves outside of the auction house awaiting Rosetta. Rosetta stepped out of a horse-drawn carriage outside of the auction house with her sisters, Georgette and Janet. They made their way inside the auction house, where Rosetta bid outlandish amounts of money on items. Eventually, a coffin was dragged on stage; Rosetta claimed that this was the very reason she was there - to purchase this coffin. As the coffin was dragged onto the stage, it began to shake and rattle violently! The chains and bindings sealing it shut began to fall off, and eventually the coffin cracked open. What might be inside? We'll have to wait until the next session to find out! That's where we'll end things for this time!

## Some things to potentially revisit later:

- **The rattling coffin** - The session's biggest cliffhanger. A coffin Rosetta specifically traveled to acquire began violently shaking, shed its chains, and cracked open at center stage. Whatever is inside, it clearly did not want to stay put. Does Rosetta know what's in it? Did she expect this, or is she just as shocked as everyone else?

- **Rosetta's motivations** - She arrived on her birthday with her sisters Georgette and Janet, bid aggressively on several items, and seemed singularly focused on acquiring that particular coffin above all else. What is her connection to whatever was sealed inside? This almost certainly won't be the last the party hears from her.

- **Peridot going off alone** - After the second Helmed Horror was felled, Peridot quietly left the party and made her way back to the beginning of the tower on her own. No explanation was given, and no one followed. Where did she go, and will she catch back up with the group? Given how upset she was about Samwell's fate, there may be more to her solitary investigation than meets the eye.

- **The teleportation circle in Samwell's storage room** - Brog completed the circle with ether chalk, but the party wisely chose not to use it while already exhausted from the demiplane. That circle leads *somewhere* - and someone clearly intended for it to be there. Worth returning to when better rested and prepared.

- **Chisum, the curiosities vendor** - Zalros agreed to visit Chisum's home to investigate some goods further. Whether this is purely professional remains to be seen - Chisum seemed quite taken with Zalros, who remained cheerfully, blissfully unaware. Either way, a private meeting at someone's home is worth being at least a little curious about.

- **Asta Vale - Elara's sister** - Jarvis promised to put his calling stone to use and dig up information on her. We don't yet know why Elara is searching for her, what happened, or whether Asta Vale is safe. Jarvis seems trustworthy enough, but who exactly is he, and how deep do his connections run?

- **The mystery scrolls (purple and green)** - Elara picked these up on the cheap precisely because nobody knows what they do. Whatever effects they hold, the moment they get used is bound to be interesting - or chaotic, or both.

- **The Kiln and the Temple of the Jeering Sparrow** - Samwell sent Zalros a map shaped suspiciously like his own birthsign, tracing the path of a centuries-old walking golem that houses a temple to the very deity Zalros is bound to. This feels less like a coincidence and more like an invitation. The party knows it's out there - it's only a matter of when they decide to go looking.

- **The Sunderland boy's goose** - A young boy and his father are feeding a goose cockatrice eggs to fatten it up for a local contest. Cockatrice eggs. Fed to a goose. For a contest. This may resolve itself without incident - or it may not, and the party may want to remember this one.

- **The sealed dugout** - The party's original job site was barricaded and marked off by the guild before they could even reach it. What happened there while they were away in the demiplane? The guild sealed it for a reason, and nobody has explained why.

- **Brog's farm** - He promised his mother he'd help put things right, and she'd been stretched thin managing the farm alone while the party was gone. Local kids causing trouble, animals to tend to, and now a new truffle pig to settle in - Brog has a home life that isn't going to wait forever.
